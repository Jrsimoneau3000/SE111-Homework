Bolt & Finding Nemo

REVIEW

Jason Simoneau April 22, 2026

Bolt is a fun and heartwarming animated film that is easy to enjoy for all ages. The story of a dog who thinks his TV superpowers are real is a clever premise, and the film makes good use of it. John Travolta does a great job giving Bolt a genuine and sincere personality, and you root for him throughout the whole journey. The humor is light and never mean-spirited, which makes it a great family movie.

What really makes Bolt stand out is the supporting characters. Rhino the hamster is one of the funniest sidekicks in any Disney movie, and Mittens the cat has a surprisingly emotional backstory that gives the film real depth. By the end, when Bolt finally makes it back to Penny, it is genuinely touching. Bolt is an underrated Disney film that deserves more recognition than it gets.

Trivia

  • Mark Walton, who voiced Rhino, was originally just a story artist whose placeholder voice was kept for the final film.
  • Bolt was the first Disney animated film produced under the creative direction of John Lasseter after Pixar merged with Disney Animation.
  • The film was originally titled "American Dog" before going through a major creative overhaul.
Jason Simoneau April 22, 2026

Finding Nemo is one of the greatest animated films ever made. The story of a father crossing the entire ocean to find his son is simple but powerful, and Pixar tells it with incredible creativity and heart. The underwater world they created is beautiful and full of imagination. Every character the audience meets along the way — from the sharks to the sea turtles — feels memorable and unique.

The relationship between Marlin and Dory is the heart of Finding Nemo. Ellen DeGeneres brings so much warmth and humor to Dory that she became one of the most beloved animated characters of all time. The film also handles real themes like overprotection, fear, and letting go in a way that works for both kids and adults. Finding Nemo is a timeless classic that holds up perfectly even today.

Trivia

  • Finding Nemo won the Academy Award for Best Animated Feature in 2004.
  • Pixar animators took a six-month scuba diving course to accurately depict the underwater world.
  • The film held the record as the best-selling DVD of all time for several years after its release.
